Why equal variances are assumed for the pooled two-sample t-test

This illustration keeps the population means equal and changes only the variances. It compares a case that violates the pooled t-test assumption (low SD vs high SD) with a clean reference case where both samples come from the same intermediate-SD population. Repeated sampling shows that large absolute t-values occur more often when the pooled equal-variance assumption is false.
