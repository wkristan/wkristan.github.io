Statistical testing as Signal vs. Noise

When we test hypotheses, we try to distinguish real effects from random sampling variation. Test statistics (like t or F) are usually expressed as some sort of signal to noise ratio, where bigger values indicate a strong signal compared with the random variation considered to be statistical "noise".

Signal intensity (size of response) 100%

Lower values are analogous to small responses to treatment (e.g. small differences between treatment and control means)

Noise level (random variation) 0

Higher values add more "static interference" to the image, which simulates the effect that random sampling variation has on our ability to detect the response.
